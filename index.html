DRNav: Semantic-Geometric Representations for Proactive Dead-End Recovery and Navigation

Abstract

We present DRNav (Dead-End Recovery-aware Nav- igation), a proactive navigation framework for environments with blocked junctions, cul-de-sacs, and occlusions. Our method fuses RGB and LiDAR features with cross-attention to estimate per- cell dead-end probability in a local semantic maps, updates these estimates with Bayesian filtering, and integrates the resulting risk map into local planning through EDE-aware (expected dead-end exposure) scoring and recovery waypoint selection. Beyond traversability, our explicitly encodes recovery points and integrates them into the navigation loop. We evaluate DRNav in dense indoor and outdoor Isaac Sim scenarios against prior methods, including DWA, MPPI, and Nav2 DWB baselines. The fused detector achieves 83.33% dead-end classification accuracy at 0.033 s/image (30 Hz), and 52.4% reduction in wasted path distance (NPR) with more proactive avoidance behavior in. dead- end-prone layouts. In closed-loop navigation, DRNav exhibits earlier turn-away behavior at dead-end approaches, reduces backtracking and oscillatory re-planning, and improves recovery after dead-end entry by selecting feasible exit waypoints.
